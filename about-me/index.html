About me

Should you wish to know

I’m currently very interested in Go (Golang), Espruino, ESP8266, IoT, Augmented reality and Web RTC. You might also know me from the concrete5 community, and maybe, way back in the day, the Dotnetnuke community. I also use PHP and JavaScript daily.

RethinkDB and IBM Bluemix are technologies I want to do more with.

I was the author of one of the first cookies disclosure scripts, which is still going. I was an accountant who used to build intranet financial systems for planning, budgeting and reporting. In 2007 I quit accounting and took up web application development full-time. I could not tell you where the time since has gone.

On holiday last year I built a course map and online scorecard for a 9 hole golf course in France, using the HTML5 Geolocation API, Google Maps, Websockets, Golang and Bluemix.

I want the future to be HTML5 apps rather than native iOS and Android apps. I’m waiting for WebRTC on iOS.

I do a lot of messing. I pick stuff up and put it down. I don’t always finish stuff. But this is how I learn. In fact most of the good stuff I’ve done in my career has its roots in something I’ve simply been meddling with at some point usually much earlier. Bigger ideas take time to come together and are usually made up of many smaller ones - stepping stones if you like.

I used to blog, and I used to do the social media thing, but I don’t so much anymore. It seems enough to build stuff, and leave it on Github and Bitbucket. What else is there to say?

You can still follow me on Twitter if you want. I’m a fairly good bet as I don’t say much and won’t get in your face. I’ll generally follow you back too, especially if you’re interested in the sort of stuff I am.
